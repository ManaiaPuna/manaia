# Manaia — How Pricing Works

## The idea behind it

Early supporters pay less. That's it. The people who show up first — who take a chance on something before it's proven — pay the least. As more people buy in and tickets run low, the price rises automatically. And eventually, every episode opens up for free. No one is locked out forever.

This isn't artificial scarcity. The price rise is real and mechanical — tied directly to how many tickets remain.

---

## Per-Episode Tickets

Each episode has 2,500 tickets. The price floor rises as tickets sell. Viewers can always pay more than the minimum — but never less.

| Tickets remaining | Price floor |
|---|---|
| 1,751–2,500 (early access) | $5 minimum |
| 1,001–1,750 | $10 minimum |
| 376–1,000 | $15 minimum |
| 1–375 (final tickets) | $20 minimum |

Once an episode sells out, it's gone until the free release date.

**EP.01 is currently free to watch** — no ticket needed.

---

## Pay What You Want

Every tier sets a floor, not a ceiling. If you want to pay $25 when the floor is $10, you can. The platform treats this as koha — giving what you're able to give, with no cap and no judgment. Many people pay exactly the minimum. Some pay more. Both are welcome.

---

## The Series Pass

One payment covers all 10 episodes.

There are 200 passes total. 63 remain.

The pass price is calculated as **75% of the sum of all current episode floor prices** — so it's always cheaper than buying each episode individually. That calculation updates automatically as tiers rise.

**Right now the pass is $34.** Buying the 10 episodes individually at their current floors would cost $45 or more.

**Your price locks at purchase.** If you buy the pass today at $34, that's your price — even if floors rise before the next episode drops.

---

## Why Does the Price Go Up?

Because demand is real and the tickets are limited. When more people buy, fewer tickets remain, and the floor moves to the next tier. There's no algorithm deciding when to raise prices — it's purely a function of how many tickets are left.

This means the first people in always pay the least. It's a direct reward for showing up early.

---

## When Do Episodes Become Free?

Every episode becomes free to watch **six months after its drop date**. No exceptions. The story eventually opens up to everyone.

Paying now gets you two things: early access, and the experience of watching as the series unfolds in real time. Waiting gets you the same episodes, eventually, for free — but you miss the ride.

---

## Gifting an Episode

You can buy a ticket on someone else's behalf. The gift can be anonymous or named — your choice. The minimum price for the tier still applies. There's no maximum.

If you want to put someone into the story who couldn't otherwise afford it, this is how.
